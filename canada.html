Key Trends in Canada

According to Statistics Canada, automation is expected to affect over 10% of current jobs. Entry-level and repetitive roles are the most at risk, while high-skill roles like data analysts and AI engineers are growing. Educational institutions and employers are now encouraging digital literacy and STEM (Science, Technology, Engineering, and Math) training.

Canada is also investing in AI hubs such as Toronto, Montreal, and Vancouver, which are becoming innovation centers for AI research, startups, and tech employment. The Canadian government is also actively funding programs for AI-related upskilling and training.
